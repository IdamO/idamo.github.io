People didn’t know what to expect from Drake’s headlining Coachella set. What they got was Madonna’s mouth hopefully not sucking out Drake’s talent.

As luck may have it though, the best could be yet to come.

Earlier this afternoon, the Zeus of internet scripture Ernest Baker dropped his profile of Drake during Week 1 of Coachella. During their conversations, the Jewish juggernaut gave Ernest the following tidbit:

I tell him that this Coachella set has to be equally transcendent. What’s his 2Pac hologram going to be?

“We’re bringing out Madonna.”

His excitement is palpable. He sort of half-punches the air with confidence when revealing the news. I’m one of twelve people who still likes Madonna, so it sounds like a great idea to me. Admittedly, the Weekend 2 surprise that he shares sounds much better.

This tells us a few things:

  • No gross mouthilingus from a rich, fading pop icon that hasn’t been worth anything to music since her turn on the Bond Soundtrack
  • East Coast individuals need to be up at 2am on a Sunday again for the tweeting, IGing, Snapchatting, Meerkatting, and Periscoping.
  • Drake will be doing something that many of his fans won’t get to watch, as this week’s stream is only on the rarefied AXS.TV network, and Drake currently isn’t scheduled to be streamed.
  • DRAKE’S GOT SOME SHIT. WILL HE BRING OUT FUTURE TO PERFORM THROW AWAY AND PROJECT PICS OF THEIR EXES ON-SCREEN? I WANT THEM TO DO THAT.

That would be awesome if it were the only morsel derived from Ernest’s time with Coachella God, but there was more.

VIEWS FROM THE 6 DETAILS.

Arguably the most anticipated album since Moses parted the seas for the slaves, Drake let loose that there are two songs that are complete for Views From The 6. One features Beyoncé. One is Left AF:

The topic of our talk switches to Drake’s upcoming fourth album. He only has two songs finished that he likes. One is a collaboration with Beyoncé that he recorded a while ago. The other is a song that he expects to inspire a paradigm shift. His days of releasing lay-up bangers that everyone loves right away are over. For now.

Views From The 6 needs to shock listeners just as much as it needs to impress them. I compare it to what Kanye did on his own fourth album, 808s & Heartbreak. Drake agrees and, as if to reinforce that notion, he tells me that Boi-1da said he needs to listen to the non-Beyoncé song a few more times before he can decide whether it’s good or not. It appears that Drake is ready to deviate from his winning formula and get a little weird.

Shoutout to Ernest Baker for doing copious drugs while orbiting and conversing with the best artist in music today. Read his entire profile of the 6 God here.
